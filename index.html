Description

Palanok Castle, located on a hill in the city of Mukachevo, is one of the most significant historical landmarks of the Zakarpattia region. Its construction began in the 11th century and continued over several centuries, resulting in an architectural ensemble that combines various styles. The castle consists of three levels — the Upper, Middle, and Lower courtyards, surrounded by massive walls and moats. Throughout its history, the castle served as a defensive fortress, the residence of Hungarian nobles, and even a prison. Today, Palanok Castle is a museum that attracts tourists with its exhibitions, towers, and breathtaking panoramic views of Zakarpattia. Visitors can explore the region's rich history and enjoy the medieval atmosphere of the fortress.
